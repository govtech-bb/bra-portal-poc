What kinds of income did you have in 2025?

Choose everything that applies. We’ll only ask you about the income types you choose — you can skip the rest.

What kinds of income did you have?

A regular salary or wage. Tax is normally taken out by your employer.

A pension from a former employer, the National Insurance Scheme (NIS), or a private pension fund.

Freelance work, a business you run, or rent from commercial property.

Rent from a house, apartment, or other residential property you own and let out.

Dividends, royalties, interest, NIS benefits, settlement payments, or gains from selling shares or government bonds.

Dividends, interest, annuities, royalties or a pension from a foreign source.

Money you earned from cane, ground provisions, livestock or fishing.

Income from a project that has been approved under the Cultural Industries Act.

advanced

Only choose this if your business earned money in a foreign currency and you want to claim the special rebate under Schedules Four and Five of the Income Tax Act.

Choose this if you received a refund of pension contributions during 2025 and want it taxed using the alternate calculation.

Not sure?

If you only had pay from an employer (PAYE) and nothing else, leave only the first option checked. That covers most people.
